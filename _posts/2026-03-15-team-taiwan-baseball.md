    There's a particular kind of joy that only baseball can give you — the slow build of tension across innings, the burst of emotion on a decisive play, and the shared heartbeat of an entire island watching together. For me, that feeling crystallized during the <strong style="color: #fbbf24;">2024 Premier 12 tournament</strong>, when Team Taiwan brought home the championship.
  </p>

## The Premier 12 Moment

<div style="background: #fffbeb; border: 2px solid #f59e0b; border-left: 6px solid #f59e0b; padding: 20px; border-radius: 10px; margin: 20px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #92400e; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 8px;">2024 Premier 12 — Champions</h3>
  <p style="margin: 0; color: #666; font-size: 0.95em; line-height: 1.7;">
    I still remember where I was when the final out was recorded. Taiwan — an island that most people couldn't point to on a map — stood on top of the international baseball world. It wasn't just a trophy. It was proof that years of development, passion, and resilience had come together. For someone like me who grew up playing baseball, who led the softball and baseball team at NTHU, that championship hit differently. It felt personal.
  </p>
  <p style="margin: 12px 0 0; color: #92400e; font-style: italic; font-size: 0.95em;">
    That tournament planted a seed: what if we could do this on the biggest stage of all?
  </p>
</div>

## WBC 2026: Games That Made Us Proud

Then came the 2026 World Baseball Classic. Taiwan didn't advance to the top 8 — I won't sugarcoat that. But two games in particular reminded me why I fell in love with this sport in the first place.

<div style="display: flex; flex-wrap: wrap; gap: 18px; margin: 20px 0;">
  <div style="flex: 1; min-width: 280px; background: #ffffff; border: 2px solid #2563eb; border-left: 6px solid #2563eb; padding: 20px; border-radius: 10px;">
    <h3 style="color: #1e3a5f; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 8px;">Taiwan vs. Czech Republic</h3>
    <p style="margin: 0; color: #666; font-size: 0.95em; line-height: 1.7;">
      The kind of game that makes new fans. A competitive, well-fought battle against a team that had already surprised the world in the previous WBC. Taiwan showed up with energy and intent, and the crowd made it feel like a home game no matter where we were.
    </p>
  </div>
  <div style="flex: 1; min-width: 280px; background: #ffffff; border: 2px solid #ef4444; border-left: 6px solid #ef4444; padding: 20px; border-radius: 10px;">
    <h3 style="color: #7f1d1d; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 8px;">Taiwan vs. Korea</h3>
    <p style="margin: 0; color: #666; font-size: 0.95em; line-height: 1.7;">
      The one that still gives me chills. The rivalry, the atmosphere, the stakes. Every pitch mattered. Every at-bat carried the weight of decades of competition between the two programs. Win or lose, games like this are why international baseball exists.
    </p>
  </div>
</div>

## Looking Ahead

<div style="background: #f0fdf4; border: 2px solid #22c55e; border-left: 6px solid #22c55e; padding: 20px; border-radius: 10px; margin: 20px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #15803d; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 8px;">The Future of Team Taiwan</h3>
  <p style="margin: 0 0 12px; color: #666; font-size: 0.95em; line-height: 1.7;">
    We didn't make the top 8 this time. That's the reality, and it stings. But here's what I know: the talent is there, the passion is there, and the infrastructure is improving. The Premier 12 championship proved that Taiwan can win when it matters. The WBC showed that our players can compete with anyone on any given day.
  </p>
  <p style="margin: 0 0 12px; color: #666; font-size: 0.95em; line-height: 1.7;">
    I believe Team Taiwan will break into the top 8 — and beyond — in a future WBC. It's not blind optimism. It's the same conviction that keeps you in the batter's box after two strikes: <em>the next pitch could change everything.</em>
  </p>
  <p style="margin: 0; color: #15803d; font-weight: 600; font-size: 1em;">
    As a statistician, I know the odds. As a baseball fan, I know that odds are made to be beaten.
  </p>
</div>
